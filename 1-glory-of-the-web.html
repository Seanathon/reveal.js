The Players

Client

They sponsor a project. Typically have their own business which you'll be helping them design (if you're lucky). Clients aren't clients until you sell them…

Salesperson

The better a salesperson is, the less they feel like one. Even their title changes, they can be called Account Executive, Business Director, even Politician. Their focus is to make sales, so it's crucial to establish minimum profit margins so that the sales made are helping the company stay afloat and profit.

Project Manager

They facilitate communication, set expectations and make sure the team and budget are on track. The Project Manager works with the rest of the roles involved to set realistic deadlines for the web design project. They routinely check in with the team and help them overcome any hold-ups or obstacles that they are facing, and relay that to the client. They also make sure all stakeholders of the project are on the same page throughout.

Content Strategist

Gather, audit and analyze existing content relevant to project requirements. Determine overall project's content requirements and sources. Develop strategy and plans based on business objectives and user needs. Taxonomies, documentation, accessibility, shepherd content, ensure plan is maintained.

Information Architect

Navigation and hierarchy creation, wireframing, labeling, taxonomies, metadata, data modeling.

User Experience Designer

It’s a design methodology rooted in a deep understanding of the user. UX design is the process of designing a solution that considers all the needs of the user. Usability testing, personas… How does the product feel to the user?

User Interface Designer

They lay out the web project's interface. They are in charge of designing each screen or page with which a user interacts and ensuring that the UI visually communicates the path that a UX designer has laid out.

Graphic Designer

Produce high-quality visual designs—from concept to execution, including those for desktop, web, and mobile devices at a variety of resolutions (icons, graphics, and marketing materials). Create and iterate on assets that reflect a brand, enforce a language, and inject beauty and life into a product.
